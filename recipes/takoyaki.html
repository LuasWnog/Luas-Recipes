Takoyaki

Ingredients

  • Katsuobushi
  • 2 green onions
  • Beni shoga
  • 120 g octopus sashimi
  • 120 g AP flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 360 ml dashi
  • 2 tbso neutral oil
  • 15 g agedama
  • 120 ml takoyaki sauce
  • Kewpie mayonaise
  • Aonori

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients

To prepare the filling

  1. Grind 3g katsuobushi into a fine powder and set aside
  2. Cut 2 green onions into fine slices and mince 1 tbsp of beni shoga
  3. Cut 120 g octopus sashimi into 1 cm pieces

To make the batter

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 120 g AP flour, 2 tsp baking powder, and 0.5 tsp sakt and whisk together
  2. Add 2 large eggs, 1 tsp soy sauce, and 360 ml dashi
  3. Whisk it all together until well blender

To cook the takoyaki

  1. Heat the takoyaki pan to 200 Celsius over medium heat. Apply 2 tbsp neutral oil into the chambers. Once smoking, add the batter. It does not matter if the batter is overflowing
  2. Add 1 octopus piece to each chamber and sprinkle over the katsuobushi powder.
  3. Sprinkle 15 g agedama, the green onion slices and the chopped pickle red ginger on top. After around 3 minutes, when the batter on the bottom has set and is slightly crisp, use skewers to break the connected batter between each chamber. Then, rotate each piece 90 degrees towards the bottom of the pan. stuffing the connected dough back into the ball as you turn it. The uncooked batter will flow out from inside to create another side of the ball.
  4. After 4 minutes rotate them again. Turn each takoyaki another 890 degrees towards the bottome of the pan. After cooking for another 4 minutes, they are done.

To serve

  1. Transfer them onto a plate and drizzle 120 ml takoyaki sauce and Kewpie mayonaise on top. Finish the dish by sprinkling katusobushi and aonori with a side of beni shoga.

Enjoy!
